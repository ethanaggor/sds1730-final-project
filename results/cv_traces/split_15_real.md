## Analysis Framework

The training data reveals several key drivers of crash severity:

1. **Appreciation (bubble size)** is the dominant predictor. States above 70% all crashed >40%; below 15% generally stayed milder than -10%.
2. **Population growth amplifies bubbles**. High pop growth + high appreciation = worst outcomes (NV: 12.18% growth, -55.69 crash).
3. **Supply constraints** decouple appreciation from crash severity. DC had 70.23% appreciation but only -11.93% crash due to stable government employment and constrained geography.
4. **Structural economic weakness** can cause crashes independent of bubbles (MI: only 8.86% appreciation but -29.40% crash from auto industry collapse).
5. **Resource/agricultural economies** proved resilient (AK: 0.00, SD: 0.00, IA: 0.00).
